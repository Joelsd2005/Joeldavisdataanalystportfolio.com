🎬 Exploring Movie Genres with Python

🧠 Project Overview

Movies are more than entertainment — they're a data-rich canvas for analysis. In this project, I used Python to explore trends in movie genres, their financial performance, and audience reception using a dataset of IMDB movie metadata.

My goals included answering:

  • Which genres are the most common?
  • Which genres generate the most revenue or profit?
  • Are popular or highly-rated movies more successful?
  • How does budget impact reception and performance?

📂 Data Cleaning & Preparation

  • Removed duplicate entries
  • Dropped rows missing genre data
  • Created a profit column = revenue - budget
  • Split multi-genre strings into individual rows using str.split('|') + stack()

🔍 Exploratory Analysis

🎭 Q1: Most Common Genres

Grouped by genre and counted unique movie titles. The most frequent genres included:

  • Drama
  • Comedy
  • Action
  • Thriller

Visuals: Pie and horizontal bar charts

💰 Q2: Average Budget and Revenue by Genre

Grouped data by genre and calculated average budget and revenue. Bar charts revealed:

  • Highest average budget: Action, Adventure
  • Strong average revenue: Animation, Fantasy

📈 Q3: Most Profitable Genres

By subtracting average budget from revenue, I found that:

  • Animation and Fantasy are highly profitable genres

These genres benefit from family audiences and long-term appeal.

🔥 Q4: Most Popular Genres

Based on the dataset's popularity metric, top genres included:

  • Action
  • Adventure
  • Science Fiction

⭐ Q5: Highest-Rated Genres (vote average ≥ 8)

Filtered to movies with vote average ≥ 8, the most highly-rated genres were:

  • Documentary
  • History

These genres, while not blockbusters, are often praised for their quality and storytelling.

🧪 Hypothesis Testing

H1: Do highly-rated movies make more revenue?

Plotted vote_average vs revenue with regression line.

📉 Result: Weak correlation — high ratings don't guarantee commercial success.

H2: Do popular movies earn more revenue?

Plotted popularity vs revenue.

✅ Clear correlation — popular movies often drive higher box office returns.

H3: Do high-budget movies earn more profit?

Plotted budget vs profit.

⚠️ Some correlation — but budget doesn't guarantee success. Risk is high.

H4: Are high-budget films more popular?

Plotted budget vs popularity.

✅ Yes — big-budget films typically gain more attention and visibility.
