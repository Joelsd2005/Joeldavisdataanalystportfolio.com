🧩 Project Objective

The primary goal was to develop a dashboard that allows stakeholders to:

  • Compare Actual vs. Budget and Forecasted spending over time
  • Identify over- and under-spending by country, date, and department
  • Provide financial visibility across departments and managerial units
  • Enable interactive exploration using filters and slicers

📂 Data Overview

The dataset includes:

  • Financials: Actual, Budget, Forecast (monthly)
  • Hierarchy: Department Manager → IT Area → IT Department
  • Geography: Country-wise spending (USA, UK, Germany, etc.)
  • Time: Jan–Nov 2020

Each data row is uniquely identified by a combination of department, manager, country, and date, making it easy to model and filter in Power BI.

🧠 Dashboard Features & Visuals

1️⃣ KPI Summary Cards

At the top of the dashboard, high-level KPIs summarize:

  • Total Actual Spend
  • Total Budget
  • Total Forecast
📌 Example: Actual spend is $258M and Budget is $289M, helping executives instantly gauge financial performance.

2️⃣ Hierarchical Matrix by Department

This matrix lets users drill down from manager to IT department, revealing who controls specific budgets:

  • Department Manager → IT Area → IT Department
👤 Example: Jim Biggs oversees both “Functional R3” and “Governance Administration.”

3️⃣ Line Chart: Actual vs Budget vs Forecast

This time-series chart shows how actual spend tracks against budget and forecast over months:

  • X-Axis: Jan–Nov 2020
  • Y-Axis: Spending ($M)
  • Lines: Actual | Budget | Forecast

It helps identify trends, seasonal spending spikes, and pacing accuracy.

4️⃣ Variance Chart (Bar & Line)

This dual chart includes:

  • Bar: Budget minus Forecast
  • Line: % difference from Forecast

Helps identify over- or under-forecasting in different months.

📈 Insight: A positive variance >30% may signal poor planning or delays.

5️⃣ Country-Level Spending Breakdown

Bar chart showing Actual, Budget, and Forecast by country:

  • Top countries: USA, UK, France, Germany, Puerto Rico
  • Unit: Billions

This enables comparison of spending across key markets.

6️⃣ Interactive Slicers

Users can filter the entire dashboard by:

  • Country
  • IT Department
  • Manager or IT Area
  • Month

This improves usability for individual department heads and analysts.

📈 Business Value

This dashboard empowers teams to:

  • Identify overspending and budget overruns early
  • Track spending patterns over time
  • Compare regional financial performance
  • Improve forecast accuracy with historical visibility

🛠 Tools & Skills Used

  • Power BI Desktop
  • Power Query for data prep
  • DAX for calculations (variance, totals)
  • Data modeling with relationships and hierarchies

✅ Final Thoughts

This Power BI dashboard transforms financial reporting into a dynamic, decision-making tool. It’s not just about visualizing numbers — it’s about enabling business users to explore data intuitively, identify problems early, and make data-backed decisions confidently.
