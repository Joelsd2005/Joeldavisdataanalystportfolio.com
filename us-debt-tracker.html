üîç Step 1: Raw Data Preparation

I started with raw data from the U.S. Treasury, which included:

  • Debt Held by the Public
  • Intragovernmental Holdings
  • Total Public Debt Outstanding (daily values)

I cleaned the dataset by:

  • Standardizing date formats

üìà Step 2: Yearly Debt Growth Analysis

To understand annual trends, I computed the year-over-year percentage increase for each debt category.

Formulas used:

= (CurrentYearDebt - PreviousYearDebt) / PreviousYearDebt

This allowed me to detect years of significant borrowing increases, often linked to economic events (e.g., 2008 financial crisis, COVID-19 stimulus).

üìÜ Step 3: Monthly Historical Trends

I examined which months historically saw the largest increases in total debt using pivot tables and formulas.

  • MONTH() to extract month
  • AVERAGEIFS() to calculate average change
  • Pivot tables to summarize by month

Findings revealed consistent growth in months like March and October‚Äîtypically linked to fiscal budget releases and adjustments.

üìä Step 4: Projecting Future Growth

Using a 5-year average growth rate, I projected debt levels for upcoming years using compound growth logic:

= PreviousYearDebt * (1 + AvgGrowthRate)

The projections were visualized using line charts to show the upward trajectory in public borrowing under current trends.

üìã Final Outputs

I summarized key findings in a final dashboard sheet that included:

  • Year-over-year growth figures

This sheet gave the agency a clear, digestible view of past and future debt trends.
